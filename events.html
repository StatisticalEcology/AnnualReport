SEEC Year in Review

2025

Executive summary

In 2025, SEEC delivered a full programme of training and community activities (symposium, short course, ecological forecasting course, research seminars, writing retreat), while also strengthening organisational foundations through re-accreditation, improved administration capacity, clearer postgraduate support structures, improved financial governance, and more formalised communications and reporting workflows.

Key events and activities

SEEC Symposium / Conference (expanded)

  • 27–28 May 2025
    • SEEC hosted an expanded two-day Symposium on Statistical Ecology & Quantitative Biology at UCT.
    • Approximately 25 abstracts were received, enabling a full two-day programme.
    • The symposium included postgraduate student and postdoctoral presentations, with participation from external research groups.
    • CoE-MASS funding support enabled the expanded format.

Ecological Forecasting Course

  • 21–25 July 2025
    • SEEC hosted a five-day course at UCT.
    • Led by Mike Dietze, with contributions from SEEC staff and collaborators.
    • Teaching resources were made available online after the course for broader access.
    • Post-course follow-up included reporting to external platforms (e.g. SAEON eNews) and exploration of future Africa-focused forecasting outputs.

SEEC Statistics Short Course

  • 27–31 October 2025
    • SEEC delivered the annual Statistics Short Course (online format).
    • The course proceeded successfully despite lower-than-usual paid uptake.
    • Participant feedback was strongly positive (reported as ~90% rating ≥4/5).
    • Course naming was refined to “Statistics (with R)” to clarify that R supports the course rather than being the primary objective.
    • Migration of course material from Vula to Amathuba was initiated following Vula’s discontinuation, with initial conversion completed in 2025.

Research support and writing

  • 8–11 December 2025
    • SEEC held a Writing Retreat (3 nights).
    • Approximately 10 participants, prioritising staff and postgraduate students with active manuscripts or thesis chapters.
    • Participation criteria were formalised at leadership level to prioritise students nearing completion.
    • Supported through CoE-MASS funding and aligned with SEEC’s focus on outputs and completion.

Community-building

  • 11 October 2025
    • SEEC Pizza Party (informal community event for staff, students, and visitors).

Organisational progress in 2025

Governance and accreditation

  • SEEC progressed through a formal review process during 2025 and was re-accredited for a further five years (until end of 2030).
  • SEEC retained a co-director leadership structure, supporting cross-departmental anchoring.
  • Leadership engagement with Faculty highlighted challenges in review processes, administration, and cross-departmental positioning.

Administration and operations

  • Nomfundo Hlongwa joined as a shared administrator (SEEC allocation: 20%) from May 2025.
  • Work continued to clarify core vs affiliated membership expectations and participation norms.
  • Development of departmental MoUs progressed (starting with Statistical Sciences) to improve continuity and stability across departments.
  • Leadership discussions explicitly addressed financial sustainability and cost recovery, including engagement at Faculty level on cost-recovery models.

Postgraduate coordination and support

  • Bi-weekly SEEC research meetings continued through 2025, with renewed emphasis on:
    • ensuring every student presents at least once annually,
    • creating more structured scheduling and tracking (e.g. shared schedule/spreadsheet identifying students who have and have not presented),
    • strengthening proposal presentation expectations for new MSc/PhD students.
  • Leadership discussed the introduction of PhD advisory committees (beyond supervisors) to support steady progress and accountability.
  • A consolidated postgraduate starter pack was initiated, collating guidance on:
    • admin pathways,
    • literature searching and reading,
    • writing support,
    • responsible AI use,
    • GitHub/version control,
    • high-performance computing access.

Funding pipeline and partnerships

  • CoE-MASS funding supported SEEC’s major activities (symposium expansion and writing retreat).
  • SEEC secured a Carnegie DEAL postdoctoral fellowship for 2026, with the award decision confirmed in 2025.
    • Leadership agreed on flexible allocation depending on outcomes of parallel funding applications.
  • Funding pipeline development progressed, including:
    • exploration of major calls (e.g. NRF thematic funding, AI–climate-aligned opportunities, IKI Large Grants),
    • continued development of applied-partnership funding conversations (e.g. BIRDIE-related discussions),
    • engagement with UCT’s Development and Alumni Department (DAD) around structured fundraising and research-chair-scale proposals.
  • Development of a SEEC business plan, aligned with MARIS-style planning, was initiated to support MoUs, major funding applications, and long-term sustainability.

Communications, reporting, and visibility

  • Website and communications responsibilities transitioned during 2025, with stronger emphasis on:
    • keeping student and staff pages current,
    • ensuring the publication list is up to date,
    • improving reporting inputs (spreadsheets) to support semi-automated reporting.
  • Social media focus increasingly shifted toward LinkedIn and Bluesky, supported by reusable templates for publications, achievements, and announcements.
  • Better tracking of conference attendance, achievements/prizes, and outputs was introduced to support annual reporting.
  • Michelle resigned as SEEC communications lead in December 2025 and was replaced by postdoctoral fellow Stephanie, who completed Drupal training and assumed responsibility for website updates and outreach.
